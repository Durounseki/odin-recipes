Bulgogi Sandwich

This mouthwatering beef sandwich was inspired by the famous Bibimbap, a korean comfort food consisting of rice, mixed veggies, either sweet soy sauce marinated beef or a spicy pork and a variety of extra toppings such as gim, and obviously tons of kimchi. For this recipe I chose to combine the flavors of the sweet beef and the spicy pork for the main protein. For the toppings I added some lettuce, cucumber and carrots. For the toppings I went for the classic Nappa cabage kimchi and a very special treat, Mayak Gyeran or as some of us call them drug eggs. You definitely have to try, here is how to make it.

Ingredients

For the bulgogi:

  • Sliced rib eye steak
  • Ginger
  • Garlic
  • Sugar
  • Chilli peppers
  • Soy sauce
  • Japanese mirin
  • Cooking sake
  • Sesame oil
  • Gochujang
  • Salt and pepper
Toppings:
  • Spring onions
  • Lettuce
  • Carrots
  • Cucumber
  • Kimchi
  • Mayak Gyeran
And of course, find yourself a good brioche, milk bread, or your choice of bread. For the moment, I am not adding the exact quantities of each ingredient, I will come back later to add more detail... or not... so you will have to do a little bit of guess work experiment and adjust to your taste.

Preparation

As for the ingredients, I will only give you some rough description of the preparation, you will have to fill in the gaps. Don't let this discourage you, I promise you this is freaking tasty.

  1. Prepare the marinade. Finely chop the ginger, garlic and chillies, add them to a bowl along with the liquids and the gochujang.
  2. Marinate the meat. In a bowl, salt and pepper the beef to taste and add the marinade. Let it rest for at least one hour.
  3. Prepare the toppingsFinely slice the lettuce and cucumber, grate some carrot and get yourself some kimchi.
  4. Mayak Gyeran. This optional, but I'm telling you this egg are highly addictive, it will blown out you head when you try them, if you cannot get them in your town or online, here is my recipe.
  5. Cooking time Stir fry the beef for a few minutes until slightly brown, cook to your taste and just before turning off the heat add some finely chopped spring onions.
  6. Build the sandwich. Toast the bread, spread the marinated eggs on top of one of the slices, add the meat, top with your veggies and kimchi. Complete the sandwich covering with the other slice of bread.
  7. Enjoy!
